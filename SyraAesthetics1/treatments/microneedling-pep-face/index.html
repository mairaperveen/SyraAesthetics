How does it work?

Microneedling is a simple cosmetic procedure that involves collagen induction therapy, a process which stimulates collagen production in your skin to initiate its natural healing process. The needles found in the microneedling pens are responsible for pricking your facial skin, being repeatedly inserted into the skin with a swift back and forth motion.

However, microneedling is not only limited to collagen production. It also promotes production of other growth factors such as elastic, which gives your skin the ability to stretch and recoil easily.

Is Microneedling safe?

Like any other surgical or non surgical procedure, microneedling also has some risks involved, but this procedure is not harmful in itself. You could develop an allergic reaction to the cream or serum that is applied on your skin after the procedure, and if you do not keep your skin clean, it could develop an infection.

So the best way to avoid experiencing any adverse effects of the microneedling process is knowing which cream might be sensitive to your skin, causing allergy; while also ensuring skin cleansing after the treatment.

What is the downtime for microneedling?

During the microneedling process, our experts move the device across your skin, creating micro-injury sites in the process. Since the procedure causes injuries to your skin, it needs time to heal. You might experience mild swelling, redness, and discomfort that might be relieved within three to seven days, different for every individual, as it depends on the typical healing pace of your body.

How Many Sessions Are Required?

The number of sessions you require depends on your skin type and how it responds to the procedure, which will be determined by our aestheticians after conducting the comprehensive consultation.

Nonetheless, typically for most people the ideal number of sessions required to achieve effective results, is anywhere from three to six sessions.

Is Microneedling Painful?

The entire notion of skin pricking with micro needles might give you an impression that this immensely effective skin treatment might be a little too painful, but contrary to the popular perception, people who have experienced it can tell you that its not.

You might feel minor discomfort during and after the procedure, but the topical anesthetic that is applied onto your skin in order to numb the treatment area, eliminates even the most minimal pricking pain and the discomfort of swollen injuries.
